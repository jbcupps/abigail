# Vision Alignment Question Packs

Prepared for post-Birth sessions:
- Chunk 02: Identity and Soul Registry.
- Chunk 03: Chat Experience Contract.

## Chunk 02 - Identity and Soul Registry

### Objective
Align how identities are created, selected, migrated, archived, and governed across home and office use-cases.

### Core Questions (Session Set)
1. Identity model priority:
   - Personal-first simplicity, or organization-first governance?
2. Registry default screen behavior:
   - Last active soul auto-selected, or explicit selection every launch?
3. Creation policy:
   - Open create-anytime, capped count, or role/policy gated?
4. Migration/conflict behavior:
   - Preserve-first, prompt-heavy reconciliation, or one-click conversion?
5. Archive/delete semantics:
   - Soft archive by default, or explicit hard delete options visible early?
6. Cross-identity resource sharing:
   - Isolated by default, or selective shared capabilities/vaults?
7. Visual identity governance:
   - Mentor-controlled only, or persona self-adaptation within limits?
8. Office mode requirements:
   - Need admin templates and locked policies in this chunk or later?

### Decision Outputs Expected
- Identity lifecycle contract (create/select/load/disconnect/archive/wipe).
- Conflict-resolution UX and fallback behavior.
- Policy model for home vs office installs.

## Chunk 03 - Chat Experience Contract

### Objective
Define exact behavior of message UX, streaming, routing visibility, tool transparency, and failure handling.

### Core Questions (Session Set)
1. Response style default:
   - Brief/operational, balanced, or deeply explanatory?
2. Routing transparency:
   - Always show provider/routing path, optional toggle, or mostly hidden?
3. Tool execution visibility:
   - Quiet background, status badges, or full execution transcript?
4. Safety posture in chat:
   - Strict refusal-first, guided alternatives, or contextual flexibility?
5. Error handling:
   - Inline corrective guidance, modal interruptions, or retry queues?
6. Configuration inside chat:
   - Keep in-line provider setup menu, or move to dedicated settings surface?
7. Multi-line input and send behavior:
   - Enter-to-send default, Shift+Enter newline, with configurable preference?
8. Memory use disclosure:
   - Always indicate recall usage, optional disclosure, or hidden by default?

### Decision Outputs Expected
- Chat interaction contract (input, output, status, and errors).
- Transparency policy for providers/tools/memory.
- UX boundaries between conversational surface and operational controls.
